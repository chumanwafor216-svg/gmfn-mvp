## 1. Purpose

This protocol defines the permanent architectural invariants of GSN.

These are principles that should remain true regardless of future features,
countries, technologies, or leadership.

They represent the identity of GSN.

---

## 2. Invariant One

GSN does not create trust.

Communities create trust.

GSN preserves, organizes, communicates, and mobilizes trust.

This must never change.

---

## 3. Invariant Two

GSN is non-custodial.

GSN does not own customer funds.

GSN records trust.

It records agreements.

It records evidence.

It records completion.

Money belongs to members and communities.

---

## 4. Invariant Three

Identity is not Membership.

Membership is not Trust.

Trust is not Reputation.

Trust emerges from behavioural evidence.

Never merge these concepts.

---

## 5. Invariant Four

Trust Passport is never manually edited.

It is always generated from evidence.

Evidence changes.

Trust Passport updates.

Trust Passport itself is never rewritten.

---

## 6. Invariant Five

TrustSlip is never permanent.

It is always generated from Trust Passport.

Different decisions produce different TrustSlips.

---

## 7. Invariant Six

Nothing important disappears.

Trust Events remain.

Verification remains.

Leadership history remains.

Community Memory remains.

History is additive.

Never destructive.

---

## 8. Invariant Seven

Community Domains configure GSN.

They never rewrite GSN.

One platform.

Infinite configurations.

---

## 9. Invariant Eight

Every important action leaves evidence.

If an action matters, it becomes an Event.

No invisible decisions.

---

## 10. Invariant Nine

Authority follows existing governance.

GSN never invents governance.

It digitises governance.

---

## 11. Invariant Ten

Every member belongs to a real governance path.

```text
Community
-> Department
-> Section
-> Line
-> Leader
-> Member
```

No isolated members.

---

## 12. Invariant Eleven

The nearest responsible leader always has greater operational authority than
distant administrators.

Trust becomes more accurate as governance becomes more local.

---

## 13. Invariant Twelve

Core engines remain generic.

Community Settings create specialization.

Never fork the platform.

---

## 14. Invariant Thirteen

Every relationship has provenance.

Endorsements.

Verification.

Approvals.

Leadership.

Transfers.

Everything must be attributable.

Anonymous trust does not exist inside GSN.

---

## 15. Invariant Fourteen

Every future feature must strengthen at least one of:

- Community;
- Trust;
- Opportunity;
- Prosperity;
- Community Memory.

Otherwise it probably does not belong in GSN.

---

## 16. Invariant Fifteen

The platform exists for communities before individuals.

Individuals are the users.

Communities are the source of trust.

This order must never reverse.

---

## 17. Invariant Sixteen

Community Memory is a strategic asset.

Leadership changes.

Members move.

Communities evolve.

Memory survives.

That is one of GSN's greatest contributions.

---

## 18. Invariant Seventeen

Evidence has priority over assumptions.

Every major decision should rely on:

- Trust Events;
- Verification;
- Community Knowledge;
- Behaviour.

Not speculation.

---

## 19. Invariant Eighteen

The Trust Graph records accountable relationships.

It is not a popularity graph.

It is not social media.

Its purpose is understanding trust relationships.

---

## 20. Founder's Constitutional Statement

If, years from now, GSN changes technologies, programming languages, databases,
user interfaces, or deployment environments, these invariants should still be
true.

These principles define the identity of GSN.

Everything else may evolve.

These should remain.

---

## Final Constitutional Declaration

Communities create value.

Behaviour creates evidence.

Evidence creates trust.

Trust creates opportunity.

Opportunity strengthens communities.

Communities strengthen society.

GSN exists to preserve that cycle across generations.

This is the permanent architectural identity of the GSN Community Domain
Operating System.

# Benefit-Led Page Explanations

Date started: 2026-04-21

Purpose: this file keeps the plain-language page explanations that can later be
polished into institutional material for mentors, innovation-school review,
partners, policy reviewers, and immigration or enterprise-support evidence.

This is not the final institutional document. It is the working source bank for
the page-level language being added to the product.

## Writing Standard

Each page explanation should describe what the page can help the user achieve,
not merely how to operate the screen.

Good page explanations should:

- show practical benefit before technical instruction
- avoid making users feel judged
- explain how the page can protect, guide, prove, or unlock something
- use simple language suitable for underbanked and unbanked members
- keep personal records separate from group or community rollups
- make privacy, consent, repair, and trust evidence clear

## Finance

Finance should not measure trust by how much money someone has. It should help
show how a person behaves around money.

This matters because a member with little formal bank history can still build
visible reliability through small completed promises: borrowing, repayment,
confirmed payments, support given responsibly, and guarantees closed properly.

Finance is the money-behaviour evidence room. It shows what happened with money
inside each community and across all communities the member belongs to.

The simplest model is:

- Finance shows what happened with money.
- Trust Passport explains what that money behaviour means.
- Trust Events record the proof that changes the trust story.

For a member who belongs to many communities, Finance should show one GSN
finance file with community units inside it. Each unit can show money in,
borrowed, repaid, still due, support given, support received, and whether that
community is Helping, Watch, or Pressure.

The human benefit is that a person who is underbanked can prove reliability from
real behaviour, not from wealth alone.

## Trust Passport

Trust Passport is not here to label a person forever. It helps a member carry
proof of reliable behaviour across communities.

One member should keep one personal GSN trust record across every community
they belong to. That personal record should gather completed money promises,
responsible support, identity continuity, community participation, repair steps,
TrustSlip status, CCI, and evidence into one explainable story.

At the same time, each selected community can still show its own smaller trust
reading. That matters because one community may see strength, watch, or pressure
before another community does. Personal trust and community trust are related,
but they are not the same thing.

Trust strengthens when promises are completed, guarantees are handled
responsibly, identity continuity is clean, and community behaviour remains
helpful. Trust comes under pressure when promises are missed, defaults happen,
overexposure appears, actions remain unresolved, or identity continuity needs
review.

The practical benefit is that the member can understand the trust story they
are carrying, see what is helping them, see what needs repair, and show a
smaller proof through TrustSlip without exposing the whole private Passport.

The simplest model is:

- Trust Passport is the fuller personal trust story.
- Community trust is the local reading for one selected group.
- TrustSlip is the smaller portable proof for one outside decision.
- Evidence packs and timelines support the story without exposing everything.

The human benefit is that reliable behaviour can travel with a person, even
when they enter a new community where people do not yet know them personally.

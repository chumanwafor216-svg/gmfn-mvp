## 1. Purpose

This protocol explains why GSN exists.

Every future decision should ultimately be measured against this mission.

Technology changes.

Communities evolve.

Markets change.

The mission remains.

---

## 2. The Original Problem

Communities create enormous amounts of value.

People participate.

People contribute.

People help.

People lead.

People fulfil commitments.

People build reputations.

Unfortunately, much of this value remains trapped inside the communities where
it was created.

When people move, recognition often resets.

Opportunity slows.

Communities lose value.

Society loses value.

This is the problem GSN exists to solve.

---

## 3. The Mission

GSN exists to help communities preserve, organise, communicate, and mobilise the
value they already create.

The objective is not to manufacture trust.

The objective is to preserve demonstrated value.

---

## 4. The Community Principle

Communities are the original trust infrastructure.

Families.

Markets.

Churches.

Schools.

Hospitals.

Professional bodies.

Cooperatives.

Unions.

Governments.

GSN respects these institutions.

It strengthens them.

It does not replace them.

---

## 5. The Trust Principle

Trust is not an opinion.

Trust emerges from repeated behavioural evidence.

Therefore GSN preserves:

- Behaviour;
- Evidence;
- History;
- Relationships.

Not popularity.

Not advertising.

Not manipulation.

---

## 6. The Opportunity Principle

Opportunity should follow demonstrated value.

Not merely visibility.

Not merely influence.

Not merely advertising.

When communities can communicate the value they already know, better decisions
become possible.

---

## 7. The Prosperity Principle

Trust creates opportunity.

Opportunity creates prosperity.

Prosperity strengthens communities.

Stronger communities create more trust.

This becomes the GSN Prosperity Cycle.

---

## 8. The Memory Principle

Communities should never lose their institutional memory.

Leadership changes.

Technology changes.

Members relocate.

History should remain.

Community Memory is a strategic asset.

---

## 9. The Human Principle

Technology should never replace human relationships.

Technology should strengthen them.

GSN exists to help communities know themselves better and communicate that
knowledge more effectively.

---

## 10. The Infrastructure Principle

Roads move goods.

Banks move money.

The Internet moves information.

GSN helps trusted value move.

This is the layer of infrastructure GSN seeks to build.

---

## 11. The Global Principle

The architecture should work equally well for:

- one family;
- one church;
- one market;
- one university;
- one hospital;
- one nation.

Scale should change.

Principles should not.

---

## 12. The Responsibility Principle

Because GSN preserves trust evidence, it carries a responsibility to remain:

- Transparent;
- Accountable;
- Auditable;
- Evidence-based;
- Community-centred.

These responsibilities should never be sacrificed for growth.

---

## 13. The Long-Term Vision

The vision is not merely another successful software company.

The vision is to establish a new category of digital infrastructure:

**Community Trust Infrastructure.**

One that enables people to carry the value of their demonstrated behaviour
across communities, organisations, and borders.

---

## 14. Founder's Declaration

Every future contributor to GSN should remember:

We are not building features.

We are building infrastructure.

We are not replacing communities.

We are strengthening communities.

We are not assigning trust.

We are preserving evidence.

We are not creating value.

We are helping value travel.

---

## Final Constitutional Statement

Communities create value.

Behaviour creates evidence.

Evidence creates trust.

Trust creates opportunity.

Opportunity creates prosperity.

Prosperity strengthens communities.

Communities strengthen society.

GSN exists to preserve this cycle, not only for today's users, but for future
generations.

This is the enduring mission of the GSN Community Domain Operating System.

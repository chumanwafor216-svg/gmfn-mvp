# Pillar of Hope Community Domain Pilot Setup

Date: 2026-07-10

Purpose: use Pillar of Hope as the first charity-style Community Domain pilot
without creating a custom charity fork of GSN.

## Setup Path

1. Open `/community-domain/purchase`.
2. Enter organization name: `Pillar of Hope`.
3. Enter requested domain name: `pillar-of-hope`.
4. Choose society type/template: `NGO / project network`.
5. Create the draft request while signed in as the responsible owner.
6. Open the created Community Domain dashboard.
7. Review the package quote.
8. Generate a payment code only after the quote amount, currency, and billing
   cycle are agreed.
9. If a payment has been made, upload proof against the generated payment code
   for finance/admin review.
10. Keep the Community Domain in draft or pending setup until admin confirms the
    payment belongs to the correct order and the amount is correct.

## Truth Boundaries

- A draft Community Domain is not active, paid, verified, or public proof.
- A package quote is not a payment instruction.
- A payment instruction is not payment confirmation.
- A payment proof upload is evidence for review, not activation.
- Activation should wait for admin reconciliation of the right payment, right
  amount, and right Community Domain order.
- The NGO template must configure the normal GSN engines. It must not create a
  separate charity platform.

## Charity Module Stance

The `NGO / project network` template should treat these as core setup areas:

- Governance
- Members
- Vault
- Spotlight
- Verification
- Analytics

For Pillar of Hope, Spotlight is useful for official messages, appeals,
updates, and reporting back to supporters or members.

Marketplace, shops, and Demand Box should remain optional. A charity may later
enable them when the real operating model needs:

- sourcing food, clothes, supplies, venues, transport, or volunteers;
- approved partner or member shops;
- trusted procurement;
- member welfare requests;
- donor-visible evidence around project needs.

Do not force member shops or open marketplace activity by default for a charity.
That should be a deliberate setting after the owner decides how Pillar of Hope
wants members, partners, and beneficiaries to interact.

## First Practical Review Questions

- Who is the responsible owner/admin for Pillar of Hope?
- What authority evidence proves that person may register the organization?
- What should Pillar of Hope call its internal units: programs, field offices,
  teams, project committees, or branches?
- Should members be ordinary volunteers, staff, beneficiaries, partners, or a
  mix of roles?
- Should Demand Box be off at first, or enabled only for internal sourcing?
- Should shops stay off at first unless approved partner/vendor commerce is
  needed?
- What payment amount and billing cycle are being tested in the pilot?

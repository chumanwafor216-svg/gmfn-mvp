## 1. Purpose

This document defines the philosophy that governs the Community Domain Engine.

It is the constitutional document for all future Community Domain development.

Every future feature, API, permission, workflow, database entity, and user
interface for Community Domains must comply with the principles contained here.

If any future Community Domain implementation conflicts with this protocol, this
protocol takes precedence for the Community Domain Engine.

---

## 2. The Fundamental Assumption

GSN begins with one assumption:

> Communities already create trust.

Markets create trust.

Churches create trust.

Schools create trust.

Hospitals create trust.

Professional bodies create trust.

Families create trust.

Cooperatives create trust.

Unions create trust.

GSN does not create trust.

GSN preserves, organizes, communicates, and mobilizes the value communities
already create.

---

## 3. Why Community Domains Exist

A normal community inside GSN allows people to organize themselves.

A Community Domain allows an existing real-world institution to establish its
official digital operating space inside GSN.

Examples include:

- schools;
- universities;
- churches;
- markets;
- hospitals;
- cooperatives;
- professional bodies;
- trade associations;
- town unions;
- diaspora organizations;
- government-approved associations.

A Community Domain is therefore an institutional representation rather than an
informal group.

---

## 4. Committee vs Community Domain

GSN supports two different kinds of communities.

### Committee

A Committee is a lightweight community created by ordinary users.

Examples:

- friends;
- study groups;
- temporary projects;
- family groups;
- hobby groups.

Committees require no institutional verification.

They are simple social communities.

### Community Domain

A Community Domain represents a recognized organization.

Characteristics:

- purchased domain;
- community identity;
- governance;
- departments;
- membership administration;
- delegated authority;
- community verification;
- community analytics.

Community Domains become long-term digital assets.

---

## 5. Community Domain Is Not A New Platform

Community Domains do not replace GSN.

They configure GSN.

Every Community Domain uses the same engines:

- Marketplace;
- Spotlight;
- Vault;
- Demand Box;
- Trust Passport;
- TrustSlip;
- Merchant Release Rail;
- Community Finance;
- Market Wisdom.

Nothing is rewritten.

Everything is configured.

---

## 6. Core Design Principle

The GSN Core Engines are permanent.

Community Domains configure those engines through Community Settings.

Therefore:

```text
Core Engine
-> Community Settings
-> Configured Community Domain
-> Member Experience
```

The platform remains one.

Communities become unique.

---

## 7. Community Settings Philosophy

Every Community Domain begins with Community Settings.

Community Settings define:

- community type;
- governance structure;
- departments;
- permissions;
- visibility rules;
- Spotlight rules;
- Marketplace rules;
- notification rules;
- verification rules.

Community Settings must never modify core engine behavior.

They only configure access and workflow.

---

## 8. Governance Principle

GSN never replaces existing governance.

Instead it mirrors existing governance.

Example:

```text
Onitsha Main Market
-> Chairman
-> Market Leaders
-> Line Chairmen
-> Members
```

The same principle applies to schools, churches, hospitals, and unions.

Authority always follows the existing organizational structure.

---

## 9. Delegated Trust Principle

GSN verifies the institution.

The institution governs itself.

Authority flows downward.

Trust grows upward.

This means:

```text
GSN verifies
-> Community Domain
-> Community Leadership
-> Department Leadership
-> Members
```

The closer a leader is to a member, the stronger their authority to confirm
membership.

---

## 10. Membership Principle

Membership is not Trust.

Membership simply confirms:

> This person belongs here.

Trust begins only after:

- participation;
- contribution;
- commitment;
- support;
- leadership;
- other Trust Events.

This separation protects the integrity of Trust Passport.

---

## 11. Community Identity Principle

A Community Domain owns:

- community identity;
- Community Domain name;
- community governance;
- Community Settings;
- community structure.

Members belong to Community Domains.

Members do not own Community Domains.

---

## 12. Community Domain Lifecycle

```text
Stage 1: Purchase Community Domain
-> Stage 2: GSN verifies organization
-> Stage 3: Community configures governance
-> Stage 4: Departments created
-> Stage 5: Authority delegated
-> Stage 6: Members onboarded
-> Stage 7: Trust Events begin
-> Stage 8: Trust Passport grows
-> Stage 9: Community becomes part of the wider GSN Trust Infrastructure
```

---

## 13. Design Constraints

Coders must never:

- create separate versions of GSN for schools, churches, or markets;
- hard-code community-specific workflows into core engines;
- allow Community Settings to alter core engine logic.

Coders must always:

- keep engines generic;
- configure behavior through Community Settings;
- preserve compatibility between all Community Domains.

---

## 14. Future Compatibility

Every future organization should be onboarded by configuring a Community Domain
rather than modifying GSN.

This ensures:

- scalability;
- maintainability;
- interoperability;
- long-term architectural stability.

---

## Constitutional Statement

GSN is a Trust Infrastructure Platform.

Community Domains are the institutional operating spaces through which
communities preserve, organize, communicate, and mobilize the trust they already
create.

The Community Domain Engine does not replace communities.

It strengthens them while preserving one universal trust infrastructure.

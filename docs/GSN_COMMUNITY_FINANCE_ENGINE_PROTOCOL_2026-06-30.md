# GSN Community Domain Engine

## Protocol 10 - Community Finance Engine Protocol

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Core Community Finance Protocol
**Audience:** Codex Developers, Product Architects, Community Administrators

---

Protocol 12,
`docs/GSN_ADMINISTRATION_COMMAND_CENTRE_COMMUNITY_INTELLIGENCE_PROTOCOL_2026-06-30.md`,

---

## 1. Purpose

The Community Finance Engine exists to help communities organize trusted
financial cooperation without GSN becoming a bank, lender, or custodian of
funds.

GSN provides:

- Trust Infrastructure;
- Coordination;
- Evidence;
- Verification;
- Accountability;
- Audit Trails.

The movement and custody of money remain outside GSN unless future regulated
integrations are introduced.

---

## 2. Constitutional Principle

GSN does not lend money.

Communities lend.

Members lend.

Institutions lend.

GSN records, organizes, verifies, and preserves the trust evidence surrounding
those activities.

---

## 3. Scope of the Community Finance Engine

The engine supports:

- Rotational Savings;
- Contribution Groups;
- Emergency Support Funds;
- Community Welfare;
- People-backed Loans;
- Guarantor-backed Loans;
- Contribution History;
- Repayment History;
- Financial Trust Events.

The engine must never become a banking ledger.

---

## 4. Financial Philosophy

Traditional financial systems ask:

> What assets does this person own?

GSN first asks:

> What trust evidence surrounds this financial relationship?

Financial decisions should therefore benefit from:

- Trust Passport;
- TrustSlip;
- Community Verification;
- Repayment History;
- Guarantor History;
- Community Standing.

---

## 5. Community Savings

Communities may create:

- Weekly savings;
- Monthly savings;
- Cooperative savings;
- Welfare contributions;
- Development levies.

GSN records:

- contribution commitments;
- contribution completion;
- missed contributions;
- exemptions;
- approvals.

Every confirmed contribution creates a Trust Event.

---

## 6. Rotational Savings

The engine should support organized rotational savings.

Example:

```text
20 members
-> each contributes weekly
-> one member receives the pooled contribution according to the agreed schedule
```

GSN records:

- membership;
- schedule;
- contribution status;
- recipient history;
- completion.

Money remains outside GSN.

Evidence remains inside GSN.

---

## 7. People-Backed Loans

Loans originate between people or communities.

GSN records:

- borrower;
- lenders;
- guarantors;
- agreement;
- repayment milestones;
- completion.

GSN does not provide the capital.

---

## 8. Guarantor Model

Guarantors become part of the evidence chain.

Every guarantee records:

- guarantor;
- borrower;
- amount;
- date;
- conditions;
- outcome.

Successful guarantees create positive Trust Events.

Failed guarantees also become Trust Events.

Nothing is hidden.

---

## 9. Repayment Events

Repayment should never simply change a balance.

Every repayment generates:

```text
Repayment Confirmed
-> Trust Event
-> Trust Passport Updated
-> Future TrustSlip Improved
```

Repayment behaviour is behavioural evidence.

---

## 10. Community Welfare

Communities may organize:

- emergency assistance;
- medical support;
- funeral support;
- educational support;
- hardship assistance.

GSN records:

- request;
- approval;
- contributors;
- distribution;
- completion.

The objective is transparency and accountability.

---

## 11. Financial Verification

Financial verification supports:

- repayment confirmation;
- contribution confirmation;
- guarantor confirmation;
- community confirmation.

Verification responses create Trust Events.

---

## 12. Merchant Release Rail Integration

Where financial agreements involve commerce, Merchant Release Rail records:

- agreement;
- release;
- acknowledgement;
- completion.

The Community Finance Engine consumes those records.

No duplicate workflows should exist.

---

## 13. Trust Events Generated

Examples include:

- Contribution Paid;
- Contribution Missed;
- Welfare Contribution;
- Welfare Received;
- Loan Agreement;
- Guarantor Accepted;
- Repayment Completed;
- Repayment Delayed;
- Financial Verification Confirmed;
- Rotational Savings Completed.

Every completed action creates structured evidence.

---

## 14. Community Finance Dashboard

Communities should be able to view:

- active savings groups;
- repayment summaries;
- guarantor activity;
- contribution participation;
- welfare activities;
- financial trust indicators.

Dashboards display evidence.

They do not hold money.

---

## 15. Revenue Independence

The Community Finance Engine should never depend upon interest income.

GSN revenue remains separate through:

- Community Domains;
- Subscriptions;
- Spotlight;
- Verification;
- Marketplace Services;
- Vault;
- APIs;
- Analytics.

This preserves the non-custodial philosophy.

---

## 16. Audit Trail

Every financial event must preserve:

- creator;
- approver;
- participants;
- timestamps;
- supporting evidence;
- verification status.

Financial history must never be silently deleted.

Corrections create correction events.

---

## 17. Coders Must Never

Coders must never:

- build banking functionality;
- hold customer funds;
- calculate loan interest for GSN;
- replace community financial governance;
- allow financial records without provenance.

---

## 18. Coders Must Always

Coders must always:

- preserve financial evidence;
- preserve repayment history;
- preserve guarantor history;
- integrate with Trust Passport;
- generate Trust Events automatically;
- preserve complete audit trails.

---

## Constitutional Statement

The Community Finance Engine is an evidence and coordination infrastructure.

Communities continue to own their financial relationships.

GSN preserves the behavioural evidence surrounding those relationships, allowing
trust to become visible, portable, and useful in future financial, commercial,
and social decisions.

Financial activity strengthens Trust Passport through evidence.

Trust Passport supports better future financial decisions.

This is the permanent design philosophy of the GSN Community Finance Engine.

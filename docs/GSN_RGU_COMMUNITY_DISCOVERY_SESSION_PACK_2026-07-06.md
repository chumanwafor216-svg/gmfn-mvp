# GSN RGU Community Discovery Session Pack

Prepared for: RGU Nigerian Society community discovery session
Prepared by: Chuma Nwafor
Date: 2026-07-06
Source plan: `docs/GSN_RGU_COMMUNITY_FIRST_CUSTOMER_DISCOVERY_PLAN_2026-07-06.md`

---

## 1. Purpose

```text
Test whether a real community recognises GSN as infrastructure for preserving
and sharing the trust it already creates.
```

Do not ask the room to believe Amara.

Ask the room to reveal how the Nigerian Society already creates trust, support,
community memory, introductions, accountability, and opportunity.

---

## 2. Hard Truth

```text
Interest is not validation.
Politeness is not demand.
A good story is not a market.
A useful TrustSlip is not a guarantee.
Community trust must not become a score.
```

If participants leave saying only "the app is nice", the session has not done
enough work.

If participants leave arguing about what the society should record, who can
confirm it, what must stay private, and which real decision GSN should support
first, the session has done its job.

---

## 3. Consent And Privacy Opening

```text
Before we start, I want to be clear about how this session will be used.

This is a discovery conversation, not a test of you or the society. You can
disagree, skip a question, or ask not to be quoted.

I will use the feedback to understand whether GSN solves a real community
problem, what should stay private, and what could be risky or unfair.

I may write down exact phrases because your own language is useful, but I will
not publish personal stories with names unless you explicitly agree later.

Please do not share private details about another person in the room. If you
need to describe a situation, describe the pattern rather than naming someone.
```

---

## 4. Opening Line

```text
This is not a product launch. I am testing whether GSN solves a real community
problem. Please challenge it directly.
```

First question:

```text
What does the Nigerian Society already help people do that would be harder if
the society forgot its own history?
```

---

## 5. Standard 60-Minute Flow

```text
0-5 minutes: Welcome, consent, and direct challenge invitation
5-12 minutes: Community problem
12-20 minutes: Trust map
20-27 minutes: Scenario card test
27-40 minutes: Focused app walkthrough
40-53 minutes: Challenge discussion
53-60 minutes: Decision test and close
```

If time slips, cut demo time first. Do not cut participant examples.

---

## 6. Trust Map

Draw:

```text
Community -> activity -> evidence -> confirmation -> sharing -> decision
```

For each example, ask:

- Who saw it?
- Who can confirm it?
- Who should see it later?
- What could make it unfair?

---

## 7. Scenario Cards

Use three cards only.

### Card 1: Nigerian Society At RGU

```text
The Nigerian Society helps students settle, meet people, find support, share
trusted information, discover opportunities, and introduce reliable people.

Much of this trust lives in memory, WhatsApp, officer knowledge, and personal
introductions. When leaders change or members graduate, some of that value can
disappear.

Question:
Could GSN help the society preserve useful community memory without turning
trust into a score or exposing private information?
```

### Card 2: Community Organisation

```text
A community organisation already knows who serves, contributes, leads, supports
others, attends, coordinates, and can be trusted with responsibility.

The problem is that much of this knowledge is informal. It may be hard to pass
on when leaders change, or hard to show safely to funders, partners, members, or
newcomers.

Question:
Could GSN preserve service and responsibility evidence while keeping sensitive
information private and human judgement in control?
```

### Card 3: Buyer Checking A Merchant Or Member

```text
Someone wants to buy from, refer, or work with a GSN member they do not
personally know.

They do not need the person's whole private history. They need enough current
evidence to decide whether to proceed, ask for more confirmation, reduce the
risk, or walk away.

Question:
What should GSN show, what should it refuse to show, and what should still
require direct human confirmation?
```

Ranking:

```text
Most real:
Most useful:
Most risky:
Most confusing:
Best next pilot:
Story to drop or rewrite:
```

Do not let the room say "all are good" without ranking them.

## 8. Demo Chain

Show only:

```text
Community Home -> Trust Events -> Trust Passport -> TrustSlip -> Public check
```

Say:

```text
This is the chain I want you to challenge: community activity becomes careful
evidence, and selected evidence can support a decision without exposing
everything.
```

Do not show:

- every admin tool;
- every finance tool;
- every marketplace tool;
- every settings page;
- unfinished screens that need founder explanation;
- features that do not connect to community evidence.

If the app fails, say:

```text
The live app is not the point of this session. I will use screenshots so we can
keep testing the community problem.
```

---

## 9. Redirects

If someone calls GSN a reputation app:

```text
That is the danger we are trying to avoid. GSN should not reduce a person to a
popularity score. The question is whether specific community evidence can help a
specific decision.
```

If someone calls GSN a bank or credit app:

```text
GSN is not a bank and should not approve credit. It may help show evidence that
a community or person can consider before making a human decision.
```

If someone says WhatsApp is enough:

```text
That may be true for many things. I want to understand where WhatsApp and memory
are enough, and where the society loses something because nothing is preserved.
```

If everyone is too polite:

```text
Let us make this harder. If you had to reject GSN, what would be the strongest
reason?
```

---

## 10. Close Decisions

End with:

- Should the Nigerian Society continue as a pilot discovery partner?
- Which one use case should be tested first?
- Which evidence type is worth recording first?
- Which evidence type is too risky for now?
- Who should be invited to the follow-up design session?

Final line:

```text
Do not sell. Listen for the decision the community already struggles to make.
```

## 11. Note-Taking Tags

```text
[REAL] real use case
[RISK] privacy, misuse, unfairness, exposure
[AUTH] who can confirm
[BLOCKER] adoption barrier
[LANG] participant wording worth reusing
[CONFUSION] idea needed explanation
[PULL] participant asked how to use it or what comes next
```

Capture disagreement, not just consensus.

Do not record private allegations about named people.

---

## 12. Decision Matrix

Score after the session:

```text
0 = no evidence
1 = weak / vague evidence
2 = clear evidence from some participants
3 = strong evidence from multiple participant types
```

```text
Community problem recognised:
Evidence value recognised:
Privacy boundary understood:
Authority model understood:
Story clarity:
Adoption pull:
Abuse risk surfaced:
First-use-case clarity:
```

Interpretation:

```text
20-24: Strong enough for a focused pilot design session.
14-19: Continue discovery, but narrow the story and first use case.
8-13: Weak signal. Rewrite the story or test another community segment.
0-7: Do not pitch this as validated. The room did not see the need clearly.
```

## 14. Backup Screenshot Pack

Use these only if the live app is unstable, the room has poor internet, or the
demo begins consuming the discussion. They support the same narrow chain:

```text
Community Home -> Trust Events -> Trust Passport -> TrustSlip -> Public check
```

Local screenshot order:

| Step | Screenshot | What to say |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Community Home | `screenshots/community-home-grouped-mobile-clean.png` | "This is the community operating surface. The point is not to show every tool; it is to orient the member inside a real community." |
| Trust Events | `screenshots/trust-timeline-route-mobile-record.png` | "This is the evidence layer: activity becomes a record with context, not a personality score." |
| Trust Passport | `screenshots/trust-passport-trust-document-smoke.png` | "This is a generated summary of selected evidence. It should help a human decision, not replace one." |
| TrustSlip | `screenshots/trustslip-holder-trust-document-mobile.png` | "This is a temporary shareable proof surface. It has boundaries, expiry, and does not expose the whole private history." |
| Public check | `screenshots/trustslip-verify-trust-document-smoke.png` | "This is what an outsider can check. It must be clear about what GSN confirms and what it does not confirm." |
| Community verification fallback | `screenshots/community-verify-trust-document-smoke.png` | "Use this only if the TrustSlip public-check image is too long or too hard to discuss in the room." |

Print or preload the screenshots before the session. Do not spend live time
searching folders while participants are ready to talk.

If using screenshots, say this plainly:

```text
The screenshots are only here to keep the conversation moving. The real test is
whether this evidence chain matches decisions your society already needs to
make.
```

Do not claim from screenshots alone:

- that the Nigerian Society has adopted GSN;
- that the app is production-ready for all student use;
- that TrustSlip proves a person is trustworthy;
- that public verification replaces society leaders, direct confirmation, or
  human judgement.

Unabated truth: the current screenshot set is enough for discovery backup. It
is not enough to prove live-pilot readiness; that still requires the selected
use case, authority rule, privacy boundary, and a working app walkthrough to be
tested before the actual session.

The next proof is outside the document:

```text
1. Send the organiser message and secure a session date.
2. Assemble the workshop pack and screenshots.
3. Run the session.
4. Write the after-session decision memo within 24 hours.
5. Choose one narrow follow-up design workflow only if the evidence supports it.
```

If this pack keeps growing before any participant session happens, that may be
avoidance disguised as preparation.

# GSN Community Domain Engine

## Protocol 15 - Evolution, Research, and Future Extension Protocol

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Future Architecture Protocol
**Audience:** Founders, Codex Developers, Product Architects, Researchers, Institutional Partners

---

## 1. Purpose

This protocol defines how GSN evolves without compromising its architectural
integrity.

It establishes the rules for introducing new ideas, new technologies, new
Community Domains, and future research while preserving backward compatibility.

GSN should evolve by extending the platform, not by repeatedly redesigning it.

---

## 2. Constitutional Principle

The Core Trust Infrastructure is stable.

Innovation occurs through:

- Community Settings;
- New Community Activity Catalogues;
- New Community Domains;
- New Analytics;
- New APIs;
- New Intelligence Layers.

The foundation remains unchanged.

---

## 3. The Stability Rule

The following should be considered stable architecture:

- Community Domain;
- Committee;
- Community Settings;
- Governance Tree;
- Trust Events;
- Universal Behaviour Categories;
- Trust Passport;
- TrustSlip;
- Community Verification;
- Trust Graph;
- Marketplace Engine;
- Opportunity Engine;
- Community Finance Engine;
- Knowledge Engine.

Future work builds on these.

It does not replace them.

---

## 4. Community Domain Expansion

New Community Domains should require configuration only.

Never platform redesign.

Examples:

- Airport;
- Police Service;
- University;
- Farmers Cooperative;
- Government Agency;
- Sporting Association;
- Trade Federation.

Each should onboard through Community Settings.

---

## 5. Behaviour Evolution

Universal Behaviour Categories should change only after:

- research;
- pilot evidence;
- institutional review.

Community Activities may evolve continuously.

This separation keeps Trust Passport comparable across different Community
Domains.

---

## 6. Artificial Intelligence

AI should support GSN.

AI should never replace community judgement.

Future AI may assist with:

- Community Health;
- Behavioural Trends;
- Opportunity Matching;
- Demand Prediction;
- Fraud Detection;
- Community Insights;
- Trust Graph Analysis.

Final authority remains with communities.

---

## 7. Predictive Trust

Future versions may estimate:

- Reliability;
- Participation Trend;
- Leadership Growth;
- Community Engagement;
- Trust Velocity.

These should always be clearly labelled as derived indicators, not factual
records.

Trust Events remain the source evidence.

---

## 8. Research Layer

GSN should maintain a dedicated research layer.

Potential research areas include:

- Trust Infrastructure;
- Community Governance;
- Behavioural Evidence;
- Social Capital;
- Network Science;
- Community Economics;
- Trust Graphs;
- Opportunity Propagation;
- Community Resilience.

The platform should support peer-reviewed research without exposing personal
data.

---

## 9. API Ecosystem

Future integrations may include:

- Payment Providers;
- Government Identity Systems;
- Universities;
- Banks;
- Employment Platforms;
- Health Systems;
- Development Agencies;
- Analytics Platforms.

All integrations must use documented APIs.

Core databases remain private.

---

## 10. Data Portability

Members should be able to carry:

- Trust Passport;
- TrustSlip;
- Verification History;
- Community History.

across devices and future versions of GSN.

Platform upgrades must preserve existing histories.

---

## 11. Backward Compatibility

Every new version of GSN should support previous Trust Events.

Historical evidence must remain readable.

Community Domains created today should continue functioning in future releases.

Migration scripts should update structure without destroying history.

---

## 12. Innovation Governance

Every proposed feature should answer four questions:

1. Does it strengthen an existing engine?
2. Can it be configured through Community Settings?
3. Does it preserve Trust Evidence?
4. Does it preserve backward compatibility?

If the answer to any of these is "No", the feature requires architectural review
before implementation.

---

## 13. Community Feedback

Community Domains should be able to propose:

- New Community Activities;
- New Governance Models;
- New Reports;
- New Dashboards.

Requests should be reviewed against the constitutional protocols rather than
implemented immediately.

---

## 14. Future Global Scale

The architecture should support:

- Thousands of Community Domains;
- Millions of Members;
- Multiple Countries;
- Multiple Languages;
- Multiple Legal Systems.

without redesigning the core platform.

---

## 15. Versioning

All constitutional protocols should be version-controlled.

Example:

- Protocol 1 v1.0;
- Protocol 2 v1.1;
- Protocol 6 v2.0.

Changes should include:

- reason;
- date;
- author;
- compatibility notes.

---

## 16. Design Philosophy

GSN should evolve like an operating system.

The kernel remains stable.

Capabilities expand.

Communities configure.

Research improves.

The architecture matures.

---

## 17. Coders Must Never

Coders must never:

- redesign core engines without architectural review;
- introduce community-specific code into shared engines;
- remove historical evidence;
- break compatibility with existing Community Domains.

---

## 18. Coders Must Always

Coders must always:

- extend rather than replace;
- preserve Community Memory;
- preserve Trust Evidence;
- document architectural decisions;
- keep Community Settings as the preferred extension mechanism.

---

## Final Constitutional Statement

GSN is intended to become a long-lived Trust Infrastructure Platform.

Its value will not come from constant redesign, but from the stability of its
core principles and the flexibility of its Community Domain architecture.

The platform should therefore evolve through disciplined extension,
evidence-based research, and configurable Community Domains while preserving the
integrity of Trust Events, Community Memory, and the Trust Infrastructure for
future generations.

---

## Founder's Note

The purpose of these fifteen protocols is not merely to guide software
development.

They establish the constitutional principles of the GSN Community Domain
Operating System.

Future features, APIs, user interfaces, and research initiatives should be
measured against these protocols to ensure that GSN remains one coherent Trust
Infrastructure rather than becoming a collection of disconnected features.

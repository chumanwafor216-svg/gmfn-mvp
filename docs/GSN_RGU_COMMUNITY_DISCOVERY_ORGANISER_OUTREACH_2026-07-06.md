## 1. Organiser Message

Use this to secure the session.

```text
Hi Chris and Favour,

Thank you for getting back to me.

I would like to run this as a focused community discovery session with the
Nigerian Society rather than a general product demo.

The aim is to understand how the society already creates trust, support,
introductions, and community memory, then test whether GSN could help preserve
and communicate that value responsibly.

I also want to test a few short real-world examples, such as a student society,
charity, church, market association, diaspora community, or local trade
verification, so we can learn which story people understand most clearly and
which one feels strongest for a real pilot.

Would any of these work?

- Tuesday, 14 July 2026, 5:30-7:00pm
- Wednesday, 15 July 2026, 1:00-2:30pm
- Thursday, 16 July 2026, 5:30-7:00pm

If another time is better for students, I am flexible. A group of 8-12 people is
enough, ideally with a mix of society leaders, active members, newer students,
and at least one or two people who will challenge the idea honestly.

This will not be a sales presentation. I want honest feedback on whether GSN
fits a real community need, what evidence people would trust, what should stay
private, what could be misused, and what would make adoption difficult.

Best,
Chuma
```

## 2. Participant Invitation

Use this after organisers agree to help recruit.

```text
Hi,

I am running a small discovery session with members of the Nigerian Society at
RGU.

The session is about how the society already creates trust, support,
introductions, community memory, and practical help for members. I will also
show a focused GSN walkthrough and ask whether this kind of tool could help the
society preserve useful evidence without exposing private information or
replacing human judgement.

This is not a sales presentation. I am looking for honest feedback, including
what feels risky, confusing, unnecessary, or unfair.

The session should take about 60-90 minutes. You do not need technical
knowledge. It would be especially useful to have a mix of leaders, active
members, newer students, and people who are willing to challenge the idea.

If you are open to joining, please let me know.

Thank you,
Chuma
```

## 9. Outreach Discipline

Do not overclaim in messages.

Avoid:

- saying the society is adopting GSN;
- saying GSN proves trust;
- saying GSN verifies people automatically;
- saying GSN replaces WhatsApp, leaders, or human judgement;
- inviting only friendly supporters;
- hiding that criticism is wanted.

Prefer:

- "discovery session";
- "honest feedback";
- "community memory";
- "useful evidence";
- "what should stay private";
- "what could be misused";
- "what would make adoption difficult".

Unabated truth: outreach is part of discovery. If the invite sounds like a
product launch, the room will give launch feedback instead of truth.

## Day 3-5: Run Interviews

Minimum useful target:

```text
5 interviews or one small group with strong note-taking.
```

For every interview, capture:

- current trust process;
- where trust evidence currently lives;
- one real decision where evidence matters;
- evidence people would record;
- evidence people would keep private;
- who can confirm evidence;
- correction/challenge expectation;
- strongest use case;
- biggest risk;
- exact phrase worth preserving;
- pilot pull reading.

Do not ask first:

```text
Do you like GSN?
```

Ask first:

```text
How does your community currently know who is reliable, who contributes, who can be trusted with responsibility, or who needs more checking before a decision is made?
```

## What To Say If Asked Where GSN Is Now

```text
GSN is at the structured customer discovery stage. The theory is clear enough to test, but the next proof is whether real communities can name a painful decision, evidence worth preserving, acceptable safeguards, and a narrow pilot workflow.
```

## What Not To Say

- Do not say RGU endorsed GSN.
- Do not say product-market fit exists.
- Do not say the first wedge is proven.
- Do not say accepted local product screenshots prove traction, adoption,
  revenue, external validation, recipient trust, product-market fit, or
  safeguards at scale.
- Do not say GSN will approve credit or guarantee trust.
- Do not say the privacy/governance model is solved.
- Do not call Behavioural Capital an accepted market category yet.

## Evidence Thresholds

Green:

- 5+ participants independently name the same decision pain.
- A credible organiser wants a pilot.
- Participants name specific evidence and specific privacy boundaries.
- One buyer/user path becomes clearer.

Yellow:

- People understand the idea but disagree on the use case.
- Participants like the concept but worry about authority or misuse.
- The first buyer remains unclear.

Red:

- Participants mostly say the idea is interesting but cannot name a decision.
- People read GSN as social scoring.
- Privacy/refusal concerns dominate the value.
- No one can explain who would use it first.

## Devil's Advocate

The packet is now stronger than the evidence. That is normal at this stage, but dangerous if ignored.

The next real milestone is not another beautiful document. It is a small set of honest conversations that produce a painful first decision, a narrow pilot candidate, and a clear reason someone would trust or reject GSN.

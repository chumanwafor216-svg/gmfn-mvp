# GSN Customer Discovery Organiser Send Package

Last updated: 2026-07-19

Use this after the Chris/RGU route is clear, or when an organiser is already willing to help open a community conversation. This is for securing honest discovery participants, not pitching GSN.

## Send Goal

Ask an organiser to help arrange a small discovery conversation with people who will describe the real community problem and challenge the idea.

Do not ask the organiser to endorse GSN, sell GSN, recruit users, or promise a pilot.

## Email Body

```text
Hi [Name],

I am sharing a plain-language GSN discovery brief ahead of the conversation.

The aim is not to sell people a finished app. I want to test whether the problem is real in the community: reliable behaviour, contribution, responsibility, leadership, trade, and support are often known by people, but the evidence is scattered across memory, messages, receipts, committee knowledge, and informal trust.

The most useful feedback is honest feedback. If the idea feels unclear, risky, unnecessary, or too sensitive, I need to hear that. If it feels useful, I need to understand the exact decision where it would help.

Attached: GSN_Customer_Discovery_Plain_Language_Copy.docx

Would you be open to helping identify 5-8 people who can give direct feedback from the community perspective?

Thank you,
Chuma
```

## WhatsApp Version

```text
Hi [Name], I have prepared a plain GSN discovery brief for honest feedback. I am not trying to pitch a finished app. I want to test whether communities really need a better way to preserve reliable behaviour, contribution, responsibility, and trust evidence without turning people into scores. Could you help me identify 5-8 people who would give direct feedback?
```

## Who To Ask For

Ask the organiser for a mixed group:

```text
[ ] 1-2 leaders or organisers
[ ] 2-3 active members
[ ] 1 newer member
[ ] 1 sceptical person
[ ] 1 person who understands money, trade, welfare, referrals, or support risk
[ ] 1 person likely to worry about privacy or fairness
```

If the organiser only offers friendly supporters, say:

```text
That is helpful, but I also need at least one person who will challenge the idea directly. Praise alone will not help me test whether GSN is safe or useful.
```

## What To Say The Session Is

```text
This is a discovery conversation about how the community already creates trust, support, introductions, responsibility, and community memory. I want to learn whether any of that evidence is worth preserving, what should stay private, who should confirm it, and what could go wrong.
```

## What To Say The Session Is Not

```text
It is not a launch.
It is not a sales presentation.
It is not a request for endorsement.
It is not a promise that the community will use GSN.
It is not a financial, lending, or credit decision.
```

## Pre-Send Check

```text
[ ] The plain-language DOCX opens cleanly in Word or Google Docs.
[ ] The attachment is the customer discovery copy, not the academic or investor copy.
[ ] No product screenshot packet or raw screenshot file is attached.
[ ] The email asks for honest participants, not supporters.
[ ] The email does not imply RGU/community endorsement.
[ ] The email does not promise a pilot.
[ ] The email does not call GSN a scoring system or reputation app.
[ ] The email makes refusal and criticism welcome.
```

## If The Organiser Says Yes

Reply:

```text
Thank you. The most useful group would include a mix of leaders, active members, newer members, and at least one or two people who will challenge the idea honestly.

The session should focus on how the community already decides who is reliable, what evidence matters, what should stay private, who can confirm records, and what could make GSN unsafe or unnecessary.

I can work around the time that suits the group.
```

## If The Organiser Is Unsure

Reply:

```text
That is completely fair. I am not asking for commitment to GSN.

The useful first step is simply a discovery conversation: do people recognise the problem, what would feel useful, what would feel unsafe, and what kind of evidence should never be shared?

Even a small conversation with 3-5 honest people would help me avoid building from assumptions.
```

## If The Organiser Says No

Reply:

```text
Thank you for being direct. That is still useful feedback.

If you are open to saying why, was the issue timing, unclear value, privacy concern, lack of fit for the community, or something else?
```

Record the reason in:

```text
GSN_DISCOVERY_INTERVIEW_LOG_TEMPLATE.md
```

## Participant Invite After Organiser Agrees

```text
Hi [Name],

I am running a small GSN discovery conversation with members of [community/name].

The session is about how the community already creates trust, support, responsibility, introductions, and practical help. I want to understand whether any of that evidence should be preserved more carefully, what should stay private, who should confirm it, and what could go wrong.

This is not a sales presentation. I am looking for honest feedback, especially what feels risky, confusing, unnecessary, or unfair.

You do not need technical knowledge. Your real experience of the community is what matters.

Thank you,
Chuma
```

## Devil's Advocate

If the organiser only brings agreeable people, the session may make GSN look better than it is. You need at least one thoughtful critic in the room. A polite room can produce weak evidence while still feeling successful.

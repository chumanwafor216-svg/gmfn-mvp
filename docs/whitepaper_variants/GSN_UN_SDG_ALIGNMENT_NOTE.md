# GSN UN Sustainable Development Goals Alignment Note

Last updated: 2026-07-19

Use this note as the source wording for white paper, grant, policy,
innovation, and investor conversations about GSN's relationship to the United
Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

## Claim Boundary

GSN should not claim to fulfil the UN Sustainable Development Goals by
declaration.

Accepted local product-path screenshots may explain how GSN could collect and
communicate community evidence, but they are not SDG impact evidence. They do
not prove adoption, measured outcomes, revenue, external validation, recipient
trust, product-market fit, or validated safeguards at scale.

The responsible claim is:

```text
GSN aligns with and may contribute evidence toward selected UN Sustainable
Development Goals when real community deployments produce measured outcomes.
```

Use:

- aligns with;
- may contribute to;
- is designed to support;
- can generate evidence relevant to.

Avoid:

- fulfils;
- proves impact;
- guarantees development outcomes;
- solves poverty, inequality, governance, or economic exclusion by itself.

## Strongest Current Alignment

### SDG 16: Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions

This is GSN's strongest development-goal fit.

GSN supports accountable community records, provenance, governance paths,
verification, correction, evidence boundaries, and decision-support documents.
It is designed to strengthen community institutions without replacing human
judgement, law, or local authority.

### SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth

GSN may support local enterprise and informal work by helping small traders,
organisers, contributors, and community businesses show credible evidence of
activity, reliability, participation, fulfilment, and trust.

### SDG 1: No Poverty

GSN may support poverty reduction where community trust evidence improves
access to support, opportunity, borrowing readiness, mutual aid, trade, or
trusted recommendations for people who are excluded from formal records.

### SDG 9: Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure

GSN is best described as digital trust infrastructure: a system for identity,
membership, trust events, verification, governance, marketplace evidence,
community finance evidence, and responsible evidence communication.

### SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities

GSN may reduce exclusion where people have real community evidence but lack
formal institutional documentation, credit files, employment records, or
conventional credentials.

### SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

GSN is community-first infrastructure for preserving community memory,
participation, membership, local commerce, support activity, and coordination.

### SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals

GSN depends on responsible collaboration between communities, leaders,
organisers, merchants, institutions, researchers, funders, and civic or public
partners.

## Secondary Or Conditional Alignment

Mention these only when a specific deployment or pilot creates direct evidence.

- SDG 4: Quality Education - if GSN supports training, financial literacy,
  community onboarding, youth development, or student society records.
- SDG 5: Gender Equality - if women traders, carers, informal workers, or
  community contributors use GSN to document contribution and access
  opportunity more fairly.
- SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production - if marketplace or shop
  evidence supports responsible local trade, origin evidence, reuse, or
  transparent production.
- SDG 13: Climate Action - if a pilot records climate resilience, community
  adaptation, green enterprise, or local environmental activity.

## Measurement Requirement

The SDG case becomes stronger only when GSN can measure real-world use.
Local screenshots, white paper language, and demo walkthroughs do not satisfy
this requirement.

Useful indicators include:

- active communities onboarded;
- members with verified identity or membership evidence;
- small businesses or traders listed;
- trust events recorded;
- successful support, repayment, contribution, or fulfilment events;
- women, youth, diaspora members, or informal traders supported;
- disputes reduced, resolved, or clarified through evidence;
- local trade or support coordinated through the platform;
- partner institutions using evidence without replacing human judgement.

## Best One-Paragraph White Paper Wording

GSN aligns most strongly with SDG 16 and SDG 8: stronger community
institutions and more credible local economic participation. It also has
plausible alignment with SDG 1, SDG 9, SDG 10, SDG 11, and SDG 17 through
poverty reduction pathways, digital trust infrastructure, inclusion,
community resilience, and partner-led deployment. These claims should remain
evidence-led: GSN does not fulfil development goals by assertion, but it can
generate measurable community evidence relevant to them.

## Devil's Advocate

Development-goal language can make GSN sound bigger than its evidence. Use it
as an alignment frame, not as a trophy. The strongest route is to prove one
community, one decision, one evidence type, and one measurable improvement
before making broad impact claims.

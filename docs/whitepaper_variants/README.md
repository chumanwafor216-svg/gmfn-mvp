Last updated: 2026-07-09

This folder contains the three audience-specific copies of the GSN Trust Infrastructure white paper, plus the send guidance needed to use them responsibly.

## Start Here

If you are about to act, do not open every file.

Use this minimum path:

1. Check the DOCX with `GSN_DOCX_SEND_READY_QA_CHECKLIST.md`.
2. Send Chris using `GSN_CHRIS_RGU_SEND_PACKAGE.md`.
3. After Chris or an organiser opens the door, use `GSN_CUSTOMER_DISCOVERY_ORGANISER_SEND_PACKAGE.md`.
4. During interviews, use `GSN_DISCOVERY_INTERVIEW_LOG_TEMPLATE.md`.
5. After interviews, use `GSN_DISCOVERY_EVIDENCE_MEMO_TEMPLATE.md`.
6. Keep `GSN_INVESTOR_SEND_PACKAGE.md` closed until discovery evidence can answer the readiness gates.
7. Use `GSN_FOUNDERS_BRIEF_GAP_CLOSURE.md` and
   `GSN_FOUNDERS_BRIEF_WORKING_DRAFT.md` before rewriting or sending any
   founder, partner, sponsor, or investor material.

The immediate next action is not investor outreach. It is the Chris/RGU send, after manual DOCX QA.

## Recommended Order

## Truth Boundary

These files are useful first audience packets. They are not final designed white papers yet. Before sending externally, open each `.docx` file in Word or Google Docs and check:

- page breaks;
- heading spacing;
- bullet and numbering appearance;
- title-page polish;
- whether the document feels too dense for that audience.

Use `GSN_DOCX_SEND_READY_QA_CHECKLIST.md` for the full manual send-ready check.

Do not send the original source white paper directly without cleaning it first. The source contained internal/editorial scaffolding and weak safeguards language. These variants were created to avoid sending that unfinished material.

## One Sentence

GSN does not create trust; it helps communities preserve and communicate the trust evidence they already recognise, with provenance, consent, and human judgement intact.

## UN SDG Alignment Boundary

The three DOCX white paper copies now include a UN Sustainable Development
Goals alignment section.

Use the careful claim:

```text
GSN aligns with and may contribute evidence toward selected UN Sustainable
Development Goals when real community deployments produce measured outcomes.
It does not fulfil those goals by assertion.
```

Strongest current alignment:

- SDG 16: Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions
- SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth
- SDG 1: No Poverty
- SDG 9: Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure
- SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities
- SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities
- SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals

Secondary or conditional alignment may apply to SDG 4, SDG 5, SDG 12, and
SDG 13 only when a specific pilot produces matching evidence.

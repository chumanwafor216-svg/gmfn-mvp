## Executive Front Page

**What GSN is:** community trust infrastructure.

**What it does first:** helps one community preserve specific evidence of
participation, support, trade, responsibility, membership, or confirmation.

**What it refuses to be:** a rating app, public reputation board, lender, bank,
automated trust judge, or social-credit system.

**First proof:** one real community uses one narrow trust-confirmation workflow
for one real decision.

**Likely first payer:** a community organiser, association, institution,
diaspora group, church/charity, marketplace sponsor, or pilot sponsor, not
ordinary members by default.

**Current honest stage:** strong thesis, source-level MVP surfaces, active pilot
preparation, but no proven revenue, no proven traction, and no completed pilot
evidence pack yet.

**Immediate ask:** help secure or sponsor a narrow Community Trust Confirmation
pilot that proves evidence creation, consent/privacy, challenge/correction,
expiry/currentness, and human-readable TrustSlip output.

## One-Page Version

GSN helps communities preserve and communicate trust evidence they already
recognise. The system should record what happened, who recorded it, which
community owns the context, who confirmed or challenged it, what privacy rule
applies, and what the evidence may support.

The first product proof should not be a full marketplace rollout. It should be a
Community Trust Confirmation pilot: one community, one organiser, one workflow,
one evidence output. That pilot should show whether real members understand the
privacy boundary, whether confirmation and challenge can be handled fairly, and
whether a TrustSlip or Trust Passport excerpt helps one real human decision
without pretending to decide for the reader.

GSN may later generate revenue from Community Domain packages, community
administration capacity, verification/evidence services, TrustSlip outputs,
trusted marketplace/shop tools, and institutional pilot packages. None of that
is proven until a budget holder accepts a narrow paid use case or sponsor path.

The founder/investor conversation should therefore ask for proof support, not
belief:

```text
What proof from discovery and one narrow pilot would make GSN a serious
community trust infrastructure opportunity?
```

## 1. Short Position

GSN is community trust infrastructure.

It helps communities preserve and share bounded evidence of participation,
support, trade, responsibility, membership, and confirmation without reducing
people to ratings, rankings, or a public score.

The first proof is not a global platform launch. The first proof is whether one
real community can use one narrow trust-confirmation workflow to support one
real human decision.

## 2. Problem

Communities already know who contributes, keeps promises, helps others, trades
honestly, carries responsibility, and can be trusted with sensitive decisions.

Today, that knowledge often lives in:

- WhatsApp messages;
- receipts and screenshots;
- leader memory;
- informal introductions;
- scattered shop, contribution, support, or repayment records;
- private judgement that disappears when people move, graduate, relocate, or
  leaders change.

The result is not only inconvenience. It means useful trust evidence is lost,
hard to verify, hard to correct, hard to share safely, and easy to distort.

## 3. What GSN Is Testing

GSN is testing whether communities will preserve and permission selected
community-recognised evidence for decisions such as:

- confirming membership or participation;
- deciding whether someone can represent or serve a community;
- checking whether a member/shop/seller has a real record;
- supporting trusted introductions;
- coordinating support, demand, contribution, or repayment decisions;
- giving an outsider a safe, limited evidence view through a TrustSlip.

GSN does not make the decision for the human reader. It provides better evidence
for human judgement.

## 4. What Has Already Been Built

Source-level MVP surfaces exist for:

- entry and community creation/joining;
- Community Domain and community context;
- Marketplace, shop, Demand Box, and Spotlight surfaces;
- Trust Passport and TrustSlip concepts;
- public verification and merchant verification direction;
- confirmation/witness flows;
- evidence-pack and pilot-readiness surfaces;
- admin and governance-facing evidence notes.

Truth boundary:

These surfaces are not the same as accepted pilot proof. Several routes and
documents still need final phone screenshots, PDF review, live-route checks, and
pilot evidence capture before they can be presented as completed proof.

## 5. First Use Case

The first use case should be:

```text
Community Trust Confirmation
```

Plain meaning:

```text
A community records that something happened, asks the right people to confirm or
challenge it where needed, protects private detail, and produces a bounded
Trust Event or TrustSlip excerpt that can support one real decision.
```

This should begin with one decision, not the whole GSN platform.

Good first decisions:

- Did this member participate or contribute?
- Did this member deliver goods, support, service, or responsibility?
- Can this shop/member be introduced or referred with more confidence?
- Can a community organiser verify a claim without exposing private records?

## 6. First Pilot

Recommended first pilot:

```text
One community
One organiser
One real workflow
One evidence output
```

Pilot workflow:

1. Select one real community event, responsibility, support, trade, or
   contribution case.
2. Record the community context and the person or shop involved.
3. Request confirmation only from eligible people.
4. Protect private contact details and private responses.
5. Store the result as a Trust Event.
6. Allow the subject or affected person to challenge or add context.
7. Produce a limited TrustSlip or Trust Passport excerpt for the agreed purpose.

Pilot pass signal:

- members understand what is private and what is shareable;
- at least one confirmation or challenge is handled correctly;
- the organiser sees a real decision become clearer;
- the evidence output helps a human reader without pretending to decide for
  them.

Pilot fail signal:

- users call it a rating system;
- members fear hidden surveillance;
- the organiser wants everything public;
- nobody can name the decision being improved;
- the workflow only works while the founder explains it.

## 7. Operating Model

GSN should separate daily work from governance.

Daily work:

- Announcements;
- Spotlight;
- Demand Box;
- Marketplace;
- Shop Diary;
- Members;
- Reports;
- Trust and evidence surfaces.

Governance:

- permissions;
- privacy;
- membership rules;
- confirmation rules;
- evidence rules;
- visibility;
- moderation;
- retention;
- security.

Constitutional rule:

```text
Features create value. Governance controls behaviour. Never confuse the two.
```

## 8. Revenue Hypothesis

Revenue may come from:

- Community Domain packages;
- community administration capacity;
- verification and evidence services;
- TrustSlip or evidence-pack outputs;
- trusted marketplace and shop tools;
- institutional pilot packages;
- later storage, member, unit, or governance capacity upgrades.

First payer hypothesis:

The first payer is likely to be a community organiser, association, institution,
diaspora group, church/charity, trusted marketplace sponsor, or pilot sponsor.
Ordinary members should not be assumed to pay first.

Truth boundary:

Revenue is not proven yet. GSN needs willingness-to-pay interviews, a first paid
pilot or sponsor signal, and proof that value repeats after founder attention
reduces.

## 9. Evidence Verification

GSN should verify information through provenance, not popularity.

Each important evidence record should answer:

- what happened;
- who recorded it;
- which community owns the context;
- who confirmed, partly confirmed, challenged, or could not confirm it;
- when it happened and when the evidence was generated;
- what privacy or sharing rule applies;
- what decision the evidence may support;
- what the evidence does not prove.

The strongest GSN record is a contextual Trust Event, not a star rating.

## 10. Consent, Privacy, And Challenge

Private evidence stays private by default.

Public or shareable evidence should not expose:

- raw private IDs;
- full bank details;
- full phone numbers;
- private contacts;
- private votes;
- private member notes;
- sensitive evidence unrelated to the decision.

Consent and privacy rules should be purpose-bound. Sharing evidence for one
decision should not silently make it public for every decision.

Challenge and correction must be available. A subject or affected person should
be able to:

- dispute the record;
- add context;
- request review;
- have the record marked contested;
- see correction history where policy allows.

The original Trust Event should remain in the audit trail, but the reader must
know when evidence is disputed, corrected, expired, withdrawn, or stale.

## 11. Expiry And Currentness

Trust evidence should age.

Old evidence should not silently act like fresh truth.

Evidence surfaces should show:

- created time;
- current, expired, withdrawn, replaced, revoked, or contested status;
- review or expiry point where relevant;
- limitation note;
- route to request fresh evidence where needed.

## 12. Anti-Social-Credit Rule

GSN must not become social credit.

Rules:

- no global human score;
- no public ranking of people;
- no permanent numerical human grade;
- no public CCI-style label as the reader-facing story;
- no automated trust judgement;
- no hidden penalty system;
- no decision without human judgement and context.

GSN may use internal evidence signals, but public/user-facing language should
translate them into descriptive evidence posture and clear limitations.

## 13. Adoption Path

The first adoption path should not be "all communities."

Best candidate wedges:

- student or diaspora community trust and introductions;
- community merchant/member verification;
- support and contribution evidence;
- association, church, charity, or society service records.

Discovery must decide which wedge has the strongest pull.

Minimum adoption proof:

- 5 to 10 honest discovery conversations;
- one organiser willing to test a narrow workflow;
- one repeated decision pain;
- specific privacy boundaries named by participants;
- a clear reason someone would use the evidence more than once.

## 17. Thirty-Day Proof Plan

This plan is not a launch plan. It is a proof plan.

### Days 1-7: Discovery Proof

Goal:

```text
Find one painful trust decision that real people name without founder prompting.
```

Required output:

- 5 to 10 interview notes or one structured session record;
- one discovery evidence memo;
- ranked first-use-case options;
- exact participant language for value, refusal, privacy, authority, and
  challenge concerns;
- stop/go decision for pilot preparation.

Stop signal:

- people say the idea is interesting but cannot name a decision;
- people experience GSN as scoring, surveillance, gossip, or public judgement;
- no credible organiser wants a narrow workflow.

### Days 8-14: Pilot Design Proof

Goal:

```text
Turn the strongest decision into one workflow with one community, one organiser,
one evidence output, and one privacy boundary.
```

Required output:

- named pilot community or candidate community;
- pilot owner/organiser;
- workflow: record, confirm, challenge, output;
- evidence fields;
- private/public boundary;
- currentness or expiry rule;
- acceptance checklist for screenshots and evidence output.

Stop signal:

- the workflow needs too many pages to explain;
- private evidence becomes public by default;
- there is no clear challenge/correction path;
- the output cannot explain what it does not prove.

### Days 15-23: Product Evidence Proof

Goal:

```text
Capture the working route or document evidence needed to show the pilot can be
tested responsibly.
```

Required output:

- phone screenshots for the chosen route path;
- Trust Event or confirmation/witness evidence view;
- TrustSlip or Trust Passport excerpt;
- limitation/currentness note;
- privacy-safe shareable output;
- rejected or unresolved gaps recorded honestly.

Stop signal:

- route evidence is too confusing for a first-time organiser;
- screenshots expose private data;
- the public output looks like a score or guarantee;
- stale, challenged, or expired evidence is not visible as such.

### Days 24-30: Pilot Readiness Decision

Goal:

```text
Decide whether to run the pilot, revise the workflow, or keep discovery open.
```

Required output:

- pilot readiness memo;
- named go/no-go decision;
- exact sponsor/partner/investor ask if ready;
- list of unresolved risks;
- next product fixes before live use.

Go signal:

- one organiser understands the workflow;
- members understand private versus shareable evidence;
- at least one confirmation or challenge path is credible;
- the evidence output helps one decision without pretending to decide for the
  reader.

No-go signal:

- first decision remains unclear;
- users read GSN as social scoring;
- privacy/refusal dominates value;
- product evidence cannot be captured cleanly;
- there is no credible payer, sponsor, or organiser path.

## Final Founder Boundary

GSN is not a rating app, social network, lender, bank, or automated trust
judge.

GSN is a community evidence infrastructure thesis. The idea is strong, but the
next proof must be narrow, real, privacy-safe, challengeable, and measured.

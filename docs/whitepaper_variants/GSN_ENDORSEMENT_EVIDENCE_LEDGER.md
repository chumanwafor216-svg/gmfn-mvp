# GSN Endorsement Evidence Ledger

Last updated: 2026-07-21

Use this ledger after every discovery, pilot, payer, safeguard, technical, or
external-review action. Its job is to separate evidence from excitement.

## Current Verdict

GSN currently has strong preparation evidence and weak market proof.

That is not failure. It is the honest starting line.

Do not use this ledger to decorate the story. Use it to decide whether GSN is
moving toward the evidence that endorsers, academic reviewers, investors,
organisers, and pilot sponsors will actually respect.

## Evidence Strength

Use one strength label:

```text
Preparation evidence - useful setup, but not market proof
External feedback - real outside response, but not demand by itself
Discovery evidence - interview/session evidence about a real problem or decision
Pilot evidence - real workflow use by a named community or organiser
Commercial evidence - payer, sponsor, price, budget, or paid-test signal
Operational evidence - proof the system works without founder over-involvement
Safeguard evidence - tested consent, refusal, correction, withdrawal, dispute, or stale-state behaviour
Technical evidence - route, permission, data integrity, audit, or test proof
Impact evidence - measured real-world outcome after use
```

## Ledger Rules

- Record what happened, not what it might mean in the best case.
- Keep screenshots, documents, interviews, pilots, revenue, and impact separate.
- Do not count accepted local product screenshots as adoption, traction,
  revenue, recipient trust, product-market fit, or validated safeguards.
- Do not count polite interest as demand.
- Do not count one enthusiastic person as a validated wedge.
- Do not mark investor-ready until first wedge, first buyer/sponsor hypothesis,
  governance boundaries, and evidence path are all clearer.

## Readiness Gate

Do not move from "endorsement-interesting" to "endorsement-ready" until the
ledger contains credible items in all of these groups:

```text
[ ] Discovery evidence from at least five interviews or one balanced session.
[ ] Repeated painful decision or first wedge.
[ ] Named organiser/community or pilot context.
[ ] Payer, sponsor, or budget-holder signal.
[ ] Consent/refusal/correction/withdrawal safeguard evidence.
[ ] External reader or recipient trust evidence.
[ ] Route/permission/audit proof for the first pilot workflow.
[ ] Operational evidence showing what works without founder explanation.
```

## Devil's Advocate

The ledger should slow down premature confidence. If most rows say
`Preparation evidence`, GSN is preparing well but has not yet proven the
business case. That is useful truth. Do not dilute it.

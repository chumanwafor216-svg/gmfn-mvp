# GSN Founder's Brief Gap Closure

Last updated: 2026-07-19

Use this as the missing-answer sheet before sending or rewriting the founder,
partner, or investor brief.

Unabated truth: this document does not prove traction, revenue, privacy
completion, or pilot readiness. It defines the honest answers GSN can give now
and the proof still required.

Current product-evidence status: a local 12-step product-path screenshot
sequence has been accepted for one synthetic demo path. That is useful evidence
that the product story can be shown, but it is not traction, adoption, revenue,
external validation, recipient trust, product-market fit, or validated
safeguards at scale.

## One-Line Founder Position

GSN is testing community trust infrastructure: a way for communities to preserve
specific, consent-aware evidence of participation, support, trade,
responsibility, and confirmation without reducing people to ratings or a public
score.

## Recommended First Pilot

Start with **Community Trust Confirmation**, not the whole marketplace.

Pilot shape:

```text
One community
One organiser
One narrow decision
One bounded evidence workflow
One Trust Event trail
One shareable proof output
```

Candidate decision:

```text
Can this member's participation, support, trade delivery, contribution, or
responsibility be confirmed well enough to support a human decision?
```

Minimum workflow:

1. Select one real community event or responsibility.
2. Record who the event concerns and which community owns the context.
3. Request confirmation only from eligible people.
4. Protect private contact details and private responses.
5. Store the result as a Trust Event.
6. Let the subject see or challenge the record.
7. Produce a limited TrustSlip or Trust Passport excerpt for the agreed purpose.

Pass signal:

- organiser can explain the value without founder translation;
- at least five members understand what is private and what is shareable;
- at least one confirmation or challenge is handled correctly;
- the evidence output helps one real decision without claiming to decide for the
  human reader.

Fail signal:

- participants call it a rating system;
- members fear hidden surveillance;
- no one can name the real decision it improves;
- the organiser wants all records public;
- the workflow only works while the founder explains it.

## Founder Brief Insert

Use this language in the brief:

```text
GSN does not rate people.

GSN records bounded Trust Events: what happened, who recorded it, which
community owns the context, who confirmed or challenged it, what privacy rule
applies, and what purpose the evidence may support.

The first pilot is not a full marketplace rollout. It is a narrow community
trust confirmation workflow that tests whether one real community can create,
protect, challenge, and share selected evidence for one real decision.
```

## What Not To Claim

- Do not claim product-market fit.
- Do not claim paying customers until payment or signed intent exists.
- Do not claim the pilot is complete just because the local 12-step product-path
  screenshot sequence is accepted; real pilot evidence still has to be
  captured with real users, consent, challenge/currentness handling, and review.
- Do not claim automated credit, lending approval, guaranteed trust, or bank
  replacement.
- Do not claim privacy is solved; say the privacy model is a pilot-critical
  design and proof requirement.
- Do not use public numerical trust scores, human grades, or CCI-style labels as
  the reader-facing story.

```text
workflow with real members, real consent boundaries, challenge handling, and a
shareable evidence output.
```

### Institutional Sponsor

```text
We are looking for a sponsor to support a bounded pilot that tests whether
community-recognised evidence can improve one real membership, support, trade,
or verification decision without becoming social scoring.
```

### Investor Or Venture Advisor

```text
We are not asking you to treat GSN as product-market fit. We are asking what
proof from discovery and one narrow pilot would make this a serious trust
infrastructure investment conversation.
```

## Decision Rule

If the brief cannot name the first payer, first use case, first pilot, privacy
boundary, challenge route, expiry/currentness rule, and anti-social-credit rule,
do not send it as an investor-ready document. Use it as an internal founder
brief and continue discovery.
